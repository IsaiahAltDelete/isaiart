The Species

An Overview of Sims

Sims are a humanoid species from a parallel material plane known as The Neighborhood — a reality operating under fundamentally different physical laws than Faerûn. In their world, needs are quantified, emotions are color-coded, and a glowing diamond floats above every living creature's head at all times.

A Note on Lifespan

Sims age in distinct life stages. In their home world an adult Sim lives approximately 24 in-world days — roughly 90 days total from infant to elder. Planar travel has disrupted this cycle. A Sim in Faerûn may age at standard Sim rates, at Faerûnian human rates, or at no rate at all. They do not know which. They are choosing not to ask.

Life Stages

The Seven Ages of a Sim

Unlike the long slow aging of elves, Sims progress through clean discrete stages. The transition is instantaneous and involves a brief light show nobody fully understands.

👶
Days 0–3

Infant

Immobile. Cannot speak. Requires constant care. Will cry in Simlish at frequencies that disturb nearby wizards.

🍼
Days 3–7

Toddler

Walks, climbs, falls frequently. Learns one Skill permanently. Will eat anything.

🧒
Days 7–20

Child

Attends school. Develops Personality traits. Unable to WooHoo, unable to die of Old Age.

📚
Days 20–34

Teen

Moody by default. Plumbob cycles at 3× rate. Part-time jobs available. Social need doubles.

🧑
Days 34–58

Young Adult

Peak condition. All careers accessible. All WooHoo unlocked. Most Sim adventurers are this stage.

👔
Days 58–82

Adult

Established. Skills peak. Slight physical softening. Sims refuse to notice this stage.

👴
Days 82–90

Elder

Grey hair, slower movement. +2 INT and WIS. Dies of Old Age at unpredictable moment with trumpet fanfare.

Mechanical Translation

For D&D purposes, Young Adult/Adult is the default playable range. Teen stage: +2 CHA but emotional penalties doubled. Elder stage: +2 INT/WIS, -2 CON, may die of Old Age on a DM-determined timer.

The Plumbob

The Diamond Above

A translucent diamond-shaped gem floats approximately one foot above the head of every Sim at all times. It is not jewelry. It is not magical in any Faerûnian sense. It simply is.

The Plumbob shifts color to reflect the Sim's emotional state. It cannot be hidden, covered, dispelled, or removed. Attempts have all failed and some produced Wild Magic side effects. Detect Magic reveals it as conjuration of a school unknown to Faerûnian theory. True Sight reveals it as part of the Sim's fundamental essence — as integral as a heart or shadow.

All creatures within 60 feet can see the Plumbob. Enemies can read the Sim's emotional state before the first attack lands. This is a significant tactical disadvantage. The Sim cannot perceive their own Plumbob. They are the only being who cannot see it.

Mood Sight — Racial Gift

The Sim can perceive the emotional state of every creature they look at, even those without Plumbobs. They automatically know when someone is lying, aroused, frightened, or grieving. This is not Detect Thoughts. It is simply how Sims perceive other beings. The Sim assumes this is normal and cannot understand why mortals make such a big deal about Insight checks.

Emotional States

The Nine Moods

The Plumbob cycles through nine documented emotional states. Each produces distinct mechanical effects.

Happy

+1 to all ability checks. Default adventuring state.

Elated

Advantage on CHA, +5 ft movement. Plumbob glows visibly.

Fine

No effect. Baseline neutral. Most NPCs live here.

Tense

Disadvantage on concentration saves. Paces involuntarily.

Angry

+2 attack rolls, -2 WIS saves. Red plumbob.

Sad

Disadvantage on social checks. Sighs before speaking.

Flirty

Advantage on Persuasion, disadvantage on Intimidation.

Depressed

-1 all checks. Cannot use Skill Grind.

Mortified

Black Plumbob. CON save DC 13 or die of Embarrassment.

Sub-Races

Occult Variants

Just as elves have Drow, Wood, Sea, and High variants, Sims have multiple sub-species. These may be present at character creation or acquired through specific planar events.

🧛Forgotten Hollow

Vampire Sim

Sired, not born. Immortal. Perpetually dramatic.

Sims converted by the Dark Form. Feed on plasma (blood), die in sunlight, develop a tree of Powers and Weaknesses chosen at creation.

Mechanical: +2 STR, Sun Vulnerability (2d6 radiant/round in direct sunlight), Plasma Drain (bite attack heals self), Mist Form, Immortality. Plumbob darkens.
🔮Glimmerbrook

Spellcaster Sim

Trained in the Realm of Magic.

Sims who studied under Sages of Practical, Mischief, or Untamed magic. Cast spells using Spell Charges. Risk of Overcharge if used carelessly.

Mechanical: +2 INT, cantrips from chosen school at will. Three schools: Practical (utility), Mischief (illusion/charm), Untamed (destructive). Overcharge = Wild Magic surge.
🧜Sulani

Mermaid Sim

Island-born, saltwater in their blood.

Sims with full aquatic transformation. Legs become tail when submerged. Communicate with dolphins. Can summon storms.

Mechanical: +2 CHA, Swim speed 60 ft, breathe underwater, Siren's Lure 1/long rest, dehydrates after 24 hours out of saltwater.
👻Any World

Ghost Sim

Died, came back, did not leave.

A Sim who died and persisted. The Plumbob is fixed forever at the emotion they died experiencing. Can be resurrected via Ambrosia or Fae intervention.

Mechanical: Resistance to all damage except radiant/psychic. Pass through non-magical barriers. Cannot be Healed normally. Plumbob locked on one emotion.
👽Sixam

Alien Sim

From the world beyond the stars.

Green-skinned Sims from Sixam. Telepathic. Can disguise themselves perfectly. Prone to abducting other Sims for scientific research they conduct politely.

Mechanical: +2 INT, Telepathy (30 ft), Disguise Self at will, Advanced Brain Power, Vulnerability to mind magic.
🤖Any Lab

Servo / PlumBot

Constructed, not born.

Robotic Sims built through the Robotics skill. Don't eat, sleep, or breathe — recharge from Solar or Electrical sources. Fully sentient. Have Plumbobs anyway.

Mechanical: Construct type, no Hunger/Energy/Hygiene/Bladder needs. Needs Charge. Vulnerable to Water. Immune to poison, disease, charm, sleep.
🌱Greenhouse

PlantSim

The Gardener who ate the wrong fruit.

Sims transformed into living plant-humanoids. Green skin, leafy hair. Photosynthesize in sunlight. Cannot be hungry if there is sun and water.

Mechanical: +2 WIS, Photosynthesis (no Hunger need with 1 hour daily sunlight), Vulnerable to Fire, Druidic Speech with plants, Hunger replaced with Water need.
🐺Moonwood Mill

Werewolf Sim

Cursed by a scratch. Transformed by the moon.

Sims afflicted with lycanthropy. Transform under full moons or intense rage. Bound by Pack loyalty. Fury mechanics at emotional extremes.

Mechanical: +2 STR, +1 CON, claws (1d6 + STR slashing), transform 1/full moon, Fury when Plumbob red. Pack bond: advantage within 30 ft of ally.

Equipment

Signature Gear

Sims do not natively use swords, bows, or staves. Their equipment is tech-adjacent, emotionally volatile, and deeply impractical by Faerûnian standards.

🔫
Moodlet Solver Ray
Uncommon Wand · Sim Tech · Attunement

Sleek silver device with green gemstone and dials. Arcane focus. 3 charges/long rest. Roll d6: 1 Frightens, 2 Charms, 3 Resets mood (intended), 4 Incapacitates with laughter, 5 Grants 2d6 temp HP, 6 Swaps emotional states.

⚡
SimRay
Rare Wand · Sim Tech · Attunement

Built at Scientist career level 10. Three functions: Transmogrify (polymorph CR 1), Freeze Ray (Hold Person), Mind Control (Dominate Person). Charges recover on short rest.

🎹
Portable Instrument
Bardic Focus · Performance

Violin, guitar, or portable piano. Grants Performance proficiency. Emotional Performance: synchronize party Plumbobs for 1 minute — allies gain advantage on one save during duration.

🎮
Voidcritter Cards
Minor Magic Item · Collectible

Small creatures bound to cards. Summon as action (Find Familiar rules). Distressed by Faerûn's fauna. Occasionally refuses to return to card.

🪑
Ironing Board
Improvised Weapon · Mundane

The Sim has killed before with this. 1d6 bludgeoning. Requires two hands. One of the most effective mundane weapons in a Sim's inventory — they understand the grip.

🕹️
MySims Trophies
Collectible · Sentimental

Small figurines. Not magical. Losing one produces Sad Plumbob for 24 hours. Finding a new one produces Elated Plumbob for 24 hours. Sim will abandon a dungeon to retrieve one.

Horticulture

Plants of the Neighborhood

Sims cultivate specific magical plants. Many have translated to Faerûn surprisingly well. Druids have taken a quiet interest.

🐄
Cowplant
Grass-green carnivorous plant with a cow-shaped head and cake in its mouth. Lures creature within reach with cake then swallows whole. Restraint save DC 14 or swallowed. 2d6 acid/round. Sim can harvest milk for Youth Essence 1/week.
💀
Death Flower
Black orchid with silver veins. One petal offered to Grim Reaper reverses a Sim's death. Used in Ambrosia recipe. In Faerûn, bargaining with Kelemvor's servants substitutes.
🍎
Forbidden Fruit
Purple-black apple of Vampiric origin. Eating transforms living Sim into Vampire over 7 days. Restores full power for existing Vampires. Refused by Celestials on principle.
🐉
Dragonfruit
Bright red hybrid from multiple spliced plants. Exceptionally valuable. Grants advantage on CON saves for 24 hours when consumed fresh.
💰
Money Tree
Literally grows currency. Requires 5,000 gold and 3 days to mature. Produces 1d6 × 100 gold per harvest. One per character. Multiple plants fail. The only economic rule The Neighborhood enforces.
🍷
Plasma Fruit
Grown from sanguine tubers. Alternate food source for Vampires unwilling to feed on sapients. Tastes like iron and regret to non-Vampires.
🌺
StrangerVille Plant
Do not approach. Do not handle. Spores cause WIS save DC 15 or become Charmed by the Plant Mother for 24 hours. Faerûn should not permit this.
Language

Simlish

Simlish is the native language of all Sims. A fully grammatical complete language that no creature in Faerûn speaks. It sounds like Italian, Welsh, Korean, and Ukrainian had an argument inside a blender.

"Sul sul."
— Hello / Goodbye. All-purpose greeting.
"Dag dag."
— Farewell. More formal. Extended partings.
"Zoop!"
— Exclamation of surprise, typically in combat.
"Plerg."
— An insult. Mild. The Sim doesn't know why it bothers them.
"Woohoo."
— Intimate act. The only Simlish word universally understood across every known plane.

WooHoo — A Note

Sims WooHoo. Intimate activity with mechanical significance — increases Romantic relationship status, produces offspring, creates Flirty moodlet retroactively. Specific locations (Rocket, Observatory, Bush, Closet) produce unique Moodlets. In Faerûn the Sim still uses the term WooHoo. It has entered local slang among companions.

Party Integration

The Sim in the Adventuring Party

The Sim is a wild card by design. Their racial features introduce small chaos into otherwise serious situations. They are also genuinely useful and emotionally loyal.

Social Dynamics

The Social need forces the Sim to engage with the party constantly. The party will know the Sim better than the Sim knows themselves within three days of travel. The Sim's Mood Sight means they know exactly what every party member is feeling at all times. They are usually polite about this. Usually.

Combat Role

Depends on Career translation. Their Plumbob broadcasts emotional state to enemies — a Tense Sim is easier to target with concentration-disruption tactics. A tactically savvy DM will weaponize this. A tactically savvy player will learn to stay Fine.
